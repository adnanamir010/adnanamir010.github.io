Autonomous Rover for Terrain Exploration

Adnan Amir, Aaryaman Chandgothia, Vedant Patkar, Chirag Sharma, Varun Mittal, Divyansh Shukla, Krish Shah, Taha Aarsiwala, Tanay Shetty
Mentored by Dr. Venkatesh Deshmukh

🎯 Mission & Vision

Our lightweight, aluminum‑chassis Mars rover conquers the toughest extraterrestrial terrains—sand dunes, rocky ledges, and everything in between—so scientists can focus on discovery, not driving.

🔍 Core Capabilities

1. Precision Instrument Maintenance

Traverse to instrument panels and perform delicate operations with sub‑centimeter accuracy.

2. Fully Autonomous Expedition

Navigate between waypoints using landmark detection and real‑time ground mapping—zero human input required.

3. Reconnaissance & Payload Delivery

Scout new areas, pick up samples, and deliver them to analysis modules with a 6 kg grip payload.

4. On‑Deck Mobile Science Lab

Collect, process, and analyze biological samples right on the chassis—bringing the lab to the field.

⚙️ Key Specs at a Glance

  • Weight: 45 kg
  • Dimensions: 920 × 940 × 500 mm
  • Chassis: Aircraft‑grade aluminum, optimized with FEA (FOS ≥ 3.5)
  • Power: Li‑Po battery pack powering 12 V, 6 V & 5 V subsystems
  • Comms: Radio + WiFi, 1 km+ line‑of‑sight range
  • Vision: 3 cameras (wide, stereo, and arm‑mounted) for 360° awareness
  • Manipulator: ABS 3D‑printed gripper on a 6‑foot arm—5 DoF, 6 kg payload

⚙️ Design & Development Pipeline

  1. Concept & CAD
    Structural FEA, material selection
  2. Procure & Manufacture
    Precision‑machined parts, rapid‑print gripper
  3. Assemble & Test
    Modular wiring harnesses, bench‑test controllers
  4. PCB Design & Integration
    Custom motor drivers, sensor I/O boards
  5. Programming & Tuning
    ROS‑based SLAM, low‑level PID loops
  6. Rigorous Field Trials
    Simulated Martian slopes, sand‑pit endurance runs

🏆 Results & Milestones

  • 98% ML‑model accuracy in obstacle classification
  • Effortless traversal over inclines up to 30° and soft sand
  • “Emerging Team of the Year” at International Rover Challenge ’24
  • Top‑10 overall finish & 4th in Astrobiology Testing

📣 Did You Know?

Our five‑person team designed, built, and tested this rover in under 40 days—from first sketch to first field trial!
